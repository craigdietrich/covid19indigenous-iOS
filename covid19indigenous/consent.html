We have developed web and mobile apps that will help communities document the impacts of and their responses to COVID-19 in real time. Communities will have full control over their own data, and only share this information with others if they want.

First Nations, MÃ©tis, and Inuit communities face barriers when accessing health data. This project will enable them to collect their own data and to respond to any impacts, reducing reliance on outside governments. The project will also help communities to track any changes in wellbeing.

To begin, we are reaching out to First Nations communities in Ontario, Saskatchewan, Alberta, and Manitoba. In total we hope that at least 24 communities will participate. That said, we welcome and encourage any and all Indigenous communities to join us, no matter where they are located.

We are now testing our equipment in a pilot project with four communities in Manitoba. We anticipate we will expand our project to all four provinces and beyond starting in October 2020. The monitoring will last until at least October 2021.

We will hire local researchers in each community to help out. Local Health Directors can modify questionnaires to fit the needs of their communities. Health Directors can immediately look at the results or get us to provide regular summaries. We will pay honouraria for all completed surveys.
